# Demo Script and Shot List

Target duration: 2 minutes 40 seconds. Narration and on-screen explanation are English;
the product UI is Japanese to demonstrate presentation-language support.

## 0:00–0:20 — Start from the problem

**Screen:** Open the product home, create a product bet, and enter an actor and pain.
Briefly enter a solution term to show the intent-recovery question.

**Narration:**

> AI agents are good at technical How, but product owners still need a safe way to
> delegate from a business problem. dodai starts with who is hurting and why. If I
> prescribe a solution, it asks for the outcome behind that method before the story
> becomes authoritative.

## 0:20–0:55 — Outcomes and verification approval

**Screen:** Describe observable improvement and what the person does first and
receives last. Briefly show that product-specific language is optional. Review
the three-part verification explanation and approve it.

**Narration:**

> I describe the person, pain, observable improvement, and the experience from first
> action to result. dodai owns the operational thresholds and how to verify the
> outcome. Before implementation, I can read what I decided, what dodai will check,
> and what approval will produce—without internal identifiers or test notation.

## 0:55–1:30 — Informed GPT-5.6 generation and runnable result

**Screen:** In the keyless demo, show the generation plan with zero API requests,
cost guardrail, and explicit sample label. Consent, open the result, submit an email,
and show PASS and the aligned stakeholder brief. Briefly explain that real mode shows
at most one GPT-5.6 request for a new approved identity.

**Narration:**

> Before work begins, dodai shows what it will produce, the maximum request count,
> the cost guardrail, and whether an approved bundle exists. This judge path uses zero
> API requests; real mode allows GPT-5.6 to derive shared meaning once after consent.
> Deterministic renderers produce a working product, behavioral verification, and the
> aligned stakeholder explanation.

## 1:30–2:05 — Govern change and product learning

**Screen:** Describe a change in Japanese, show affected records and projections,
then reject it. Enter guardrail-breaching telemetry and show the layer-four adoption
action and decision history.

**Narration:**

> A plain-language change remains a candidate. I can inspect its highest affected
> layer, every downstream record, every regenerated projection, and its model budget
> before approval or rejection. Outcome evidence produces an explained continue,
> verification-change, or exit decision and retains the learning.

## 2:05–2:25 — Codex collaboration and rebuild proof

**Screen:** Open the audit mode, show the four-layer origin, then show CI and the
cache-only rebuild result from `bash scripts/demo.sh`.

**Narration:**

> I defined the product language, pains, outcomes, and constraints. I delegated the
> complete technical How to Codex. Codex derived layer-four verification, implemented
> the product with TDD, and kept every projection rebuildable from the approved origin.

## 2:25–2:40 — Close

**Screen:** Return to the product list showing the current stage and next action.

**Narration:**

> dodai is a resumable product journey: problem, outcomes, delegated verification,
> runnable projections, governed change, and retained learning. Manage agents by
> outcomes, not methods.

## Recording checklist

- Keep the final export below 2 minutes 59 seconds.
- Use audible English narration or provide a complete English translation.
- Upload publicly to YouTube.
- Do not show terminals containing keys, environment dumps, account details, or Session IDs.
- Do not include copyrighted music, third-party marks, or unlicensed assets.
