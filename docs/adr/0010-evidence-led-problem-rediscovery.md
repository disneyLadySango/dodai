# ADR 0010: Require new observation before revising the problem account

Status: Accepted

## Context

Interaction evidence may not confirm the pain described by the approved user
story. That observation is a reason to return to discovery, but one answer is
not sufficient authority to invent a replacement actor or pain. Automatically
rewriting Layer 2 would erase learning and let the agent manufacture product
intent.

## Decision

Dodai records the unconfirmed-pain evidence against the delegation attempt and
opens a guided discovery step. The current origin remains unchanged until a
person supplies a newly observed Who and Pain in ordinary product language.
Solution vocabulary is rejected before a candidate can be prepared.

The Layer 2 candidate keeps the stable Story identity, replaces its Who and Pain,
and appends a losing record containing the previous problem account and its
evidence. Candidate impact includes every dependent acceptance criterion, test
specification, and active Presentation. Existing atomic approval regenerates the
complete projection set; rejection preserves current authority.

After approval, the product bet adopts the new Who and Pain and can be delegated
again. The losing record remains part of the authoritative Story so later
candidates that repeat the disproven framing are blocked.

## Consequences

- Evidence initiates discovery but never invents business intent.
- The previous problem framing remains inspectable instead of being overwritten.
- Layer 2 stays free of prescribed technical How.
- Downstream consequences are visible before consequential approval.
- Acceptance criteria remain structurally connected; their semantic fitness must
  still be judged as part of the displayed impact and subsequent learning.
